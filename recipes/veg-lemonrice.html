South Indian Lemon Rice (Chitranna)

Tangy, flavorful South Indian lemon rice with aromatic tempering - perfect for lunch boxes or quick meals!

Vegetarian Rice Dish South Indian Quick Meal
4

Ingredients

    Instructions

    1. Cook and cool the rice

      Cook rice with slightly less water than usual so grains are separate and not mushy. Spread on a plate and cool to room temperature. Day-old refrigerated rice works perfectly.

    2. Prepare lemon mixture

      In a small bowl, mix fresh lemon juice with turmeric powder and salt. Keep aside. The turmeric gives the rice its signature yellow color.

    3. Heat oil for tempering

      Heat oil in a wide pan or kadai on medium heat. Sesame or coconut oil gives authentic South Indian flavor, but any vegetable oil works.

    4. Add mustard seeds

      When oil is hot, add mustard seeds. Let them splutter completely - they should pop and crackle. This takes about 10-15 seconds.

    5. Add dals and peanuts

      Add urad dal, chana dal, and peanuts. Stir continuously on medium-low heat for 2-3 minutes until dals turn golden brown and peanuts are roasted. Be careful not to burn them.

    6. Add aromatics

      Add curry leaves, dry red chilies, green chilies, asafoetida, and chopped ginger. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant. Curry leaves will crackle and become crispy.

    7. Mix in rice

      Turn heat to low. Add the cooled rice and mix gently to coat with the tempering. Break any large clumps with a spatula. Stir for 2 minutes to heat through.

    8. Add lemon mixture and finish

      Pour the lemon-turmeric mixture over the rice. Mix gently but thoroughly until rice is evenly coated and turns yellow. Taste and adjust salt if needed. Garnish with coriander leaves and grated coconut. Serve warm or at room temperature.
