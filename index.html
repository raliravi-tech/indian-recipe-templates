About Indian Recipe Templates

Welcome to Indian Recipe Templates (IRT), where tradition meets precision in the kitchen. We believe that cooking authentic Indian food shouldn't be intimidating or uncertain. That's why we've created a platform that combines the rich heritage of Indian cuisine with modern convenience and accuracy.

Our Mission

To make authentic Indian cooking accessible to everyone by providing recipes with precise measurements, automated serving adjustments, and clear step-by-step instructions. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced cook, whether you're cooking for 2 or 10 people, our templates ensure perfect results every time.

What Makes Us Different

  • Automated Serving Adjustments: Our unique recipe template system automatically recalculates all ingredient quantities when you change the number of servings. No more manual math or estimation!
  • Multiple Cooking Methods: Each recipe includes specific instructions for different cooking methods - traditional stovetop, pressure cooker, and Instant Pot - so you can use whatever equipment you have.

One-Pot Pressure Cooker Veg Pulao

Easy vegetable pulao made in a pressure cooker - just layer everything and let it cook to perfection!

Vegetarian Rice Dish Pressure Cooker Instant Pot
4

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice

    Wash basmati rice in cold water 2-3 times until water runs clear. Soak for 15 minutes, then drain completely.

  2. Layer ingredients in pressure cooker

    Add ghee or oil to the pressure cooker pot. Then add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, and green cardamom directly into the ghee/oil. Add sliced onion and spread evenly at the bottom.

  3. Add aromatics and vegetables

    Add ginger-garlic paste, slit green chilies, and chopped tomato over the onions. Then add all mixed vegetables and spread them evenly.

  4. Add rice and masalas

    Add the drained rice on top of the vegetables. Sprinkle salt, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala evenly over the rice. Add chopped coriander and mint leaves if using.

  5. Add water and pressure cook

    Pour water gently over everything. Do NOT stir. Close the pressure cooker lid and set to 60 kPa (low pressure). On induction stove, set to level 7. Once steam starts coming out steadily, reduce to level 3-4 and cook for 3 minutes. Turn off heat.

  6. Natural pressure release

    Let the pressure release naturally for 10-15 minutes. Do not open the valve manually. Wait until the pressure indicator drops completely.

  7. Fluff and serve

    Open the lid and gently fluff the pulao with a fork from bottom to top to mix everything evenly. Serve hot with raita, pickle, or papad.
